Here are a few extra tips to help you get the most out of your treatment with XYREM.

Stay on Schedule:
Try not to skip a night, or miss your second dose. Setting notifications and alarms can help you plan ahead and take XYREM as directed.
Use the “Plan” Setup menu to set your treatment schedule. You can receive helpful notifications and alarms to help you stay on track.
Avoid Alcohol:
Do not take XYREM if you drink alcohol. Drinking alcohol while taking XYREM can cause serious side effects including slow breathing and changes in alertness that may require immediate medical attention.
Wait After Eating:
Wait at least 2 hours after eating before taking your first nightly dose.
Turn on the “Eat by” reminder function in the Plan Setup menu to set a meal reminder notification.
Keep in Touch:
You should see your doctor every 3 months for a check-up while taking XYREM. Between your scheduled office visits, check in often with your healthcare provider via phone or email. Record your symptoms and any side effects including nausea, vomiting, or dizziness.
Use the “Track” feature to record how you are feeling every day and create helpful Progress Reports you can easily share with your healthcare provider via email or during your next visit.
Stay Connected:
Share what you learn about XYREM and seek support from family and loved ones.
Important Safety Information
XYREM can cause serious side effects, including the following:
  • Breathing problems, including slower breathing, trouble breathing, and/or short periods of not breathing while sleeping (sleep apnea). People who already have breathing or lung problems have a higher chance of having breathing problems when they use XYREM.
  • Mental health problems, including confusion, seeing or hearing things that are not real (hallucinations), unusual or disturbing thoughts (abnormal thinking), feeling anxious or upset, depression, or thoughts of killing yourself or trying to kill yourself. Tell your doctor if you or your child have or had depression or have tried to harm yourself or themselves. Call your doctor right away if you have or your child has symptoms of mental health problems.
  • Sleepwalking. Sleepwalking can cause injuries. Call your doctor if you or your child starts sleepwalking. Your doctor should check you or your child.
Tell your doctor if you are or your child is on a salt-restricted diet or if you have or your child has high blood pressure, heart failure, or kidney problems. XYREM contains a lot of sodium (salt) and may not be right for you or your child.
